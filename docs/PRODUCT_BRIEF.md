# Product Brief — The Arrival List

## One-line concept

A private baby shower portal that feels like a luxury arrival campaign: guests join the list, RSVP, receive reminders, and browse a curated registry guide.

## Why this is different

Most baby shower sites look templated and overly sweet. This should feel editorial, cinematic, and premium while still being clear for family and friends.

## Audience

- Family members
- Friends
- Relatives who may not be tech-savvy
- Guests who need reminders
- People who want gift guidance by budget

## Core user stories

1. As a guest, I want to quickly understand the date, city, and purpose of the site.
2. As a guest, I want to join the list and RSVP without friction.
3. As a guest, I want to choose whether I receive email or SMS reminders.
4. As a guest, I want to browse registry recommendations by category and budget.
5. As the host, I want guest data stored cleanly so I can send reminders and thank-you notes.

## MVP pages

### `/`
Landing page

- Hero
- Event teaser
- Primary CTA: Join the List
- Secondary CTA: View Registry
- Registry preview
- Reminder promise

### `/join`
Signup and RSVP

Collect:

- Name
- Email
- Phone, optional
- RSVP status
- Party size
- Reminder preferences
- Gift budget preference
- Dietary notes

### `/registry`
Curated registry guide

Sections:

- Most Needed
- On Sale
- Under $50
- Group Gift
- Real Ones: diapers, wipes, postpartum support, meal train, cash fund

### `/details`
Event details

- Date
- City/location placeholder
- Dress vibe
- Parking/travel notes placeholder
- Save-the-date calendar link placeholder
- Final details note

### `/admin` — future

Not included in first MVP unless time allows.

Future admin needs:

- Guest list
- RSVP totals
- Reminder preferences
- Thank-you tracker
- Purchased gift notes
- Email/SMS campaign controls

## Content tone

Use:

- “Join the list”
- “The list is open”
- “A new life arrives in June”
- “Gift guidance by budget”
- “These are the things that help us prepare”

Avoid:

- Pushy gift language
- Too many baby clichés
- Direct public references to celebrity/brand inspiration
- Overly cold or intimidating tone

## Success criteria

- Guests understand the site immediately.
- Signup rate is high.
- RSVP data is usable.
- Reminder preferences are captured.
- Registry guidance makes gifting easier.
- The site feels original and premium.
